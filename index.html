Translation architecture

The 8-Stage Compilation Pipeline

From creative intent to physical dispatch, every stage produces evidence that can be inspected, reversed, and reproduced.

  1. 1

    Input

    Intent Capture

    Natural language, SVG, or structured data becomes an explicit abstract syntax tree.parse(intent) → AST
  2. 2

    Plan

    Orchestration

    Dependencies, tools, adapters, and validation gates become an executable graph.orchestrate(AST) → DAG
  3. 3

    Simulate

    Physics

    Material constraints, tension, deformation, and collision behavior are resolved.solve(mesh, constraints)
  4. 4

    Preview

    Render

    Material, toolpath, and registration states become inspectable before fabrication.render(PBR, toolpath)
  5. 5

    Translate

    Synesthesia

    Visual state maps to haptic and auditory feedback for accessible machine awareness.map(visual → haptic)
  6. 6

    Flatten

    Geometry Unwrap

    Three-dimensional surfaces become distortion-aware two-dimensional fabrication plans.unwrap(mesh) → UV
  7. 7

    Compile

    Fabrication Export

    Adapters generate G-Code, DST, raster layers, trapping, and registration marks.export(plan, adapter)
  8. 8

    Verify

    Cyber-Physical Handshake

    Dispatch, telemetry, and post-run evidence close the loop between plan and reality.dispatch(machine, verify)
